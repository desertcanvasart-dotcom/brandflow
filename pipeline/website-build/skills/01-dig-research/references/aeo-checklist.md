# AEO / GEO Assessment Reference

AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) / GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is about being the source AI answer engines cite, not just ranking in blue links. When assessing the search landscape in the Dig Brief, cover:

## What to assess
- **Question inventory** — the real questions the audience asks an AI assistant about this category. These become the pages/sections the site must answer definitively.
- **Current citability** — does the client currently get mentioned in AI answers for their category? Search their brand + category and see who gets named.
- **Answer gaps** — questions in the category that no competitor answers well. These are the highest-value pages to own.
- **Entity clarity** — is it clear to a machine what this business is, where it operates, and what it offers? Ambiguity here kills AI citation.
- **Structured-answer readiness** — does the content lend itself to clear, extractable answers (definitions, steps, comparisons, FAQs)?

## What feeds downstream
The AEO findings here directly shape:
- Step 2 (sitemap): which answer-owning pages must exist.
- Step 3 (content): each page structured as a clear, citable answer.
- Step 6 (SEO/AEO QA): schema, entity markup, and answer structure get verified against these targets.

Keep the assessment concrete: name the actual questions, name who currently wins them, name the gap to take.
