# Project Onboarding Questionnaire — TEMPLATE

> **How to use:** copy this file into the new project's `pipeline/inputs/`
> folder and fill it in. Sections marked **[FROM BRAND KIT]** should be pasted
> from the client's records in BrandFlow (brand guidelines + knowledge base) —
> if this client has worked with the studio before, that's a 2-minute
> copy-paste, not a re-interview. Only the **[NEW]** sections need fresh
> answers for every project.

## A. Client identity — [FROM BRAND KIT]
*Paste from BrandFlow → the client's Brand page (guidelines) and Knowledge
Base (personas, competitors, strategy). If this is a brand-new client, fill it
in here once — and afterwards, enter it into BrandFlow so next time it's a
paste.*

- **Business & offer:** what they do, who they serve, how they make money
- **Brand voice:** tone, personality, words they love/hate
- **Visual identity:** colors, fonts, logo location (drop assets in `pipeline/inputs/assets/`)
- **Audience personas:** 2–3 segments with goals and objections
- **Known competitors:** names + one line each
- **Positioning / strategy notes:** anything already agreed about how they win

## B. This project — [NEW — always fill in]

1. **Why now?** What prompted this website project — what's broken or missing?
2. **One job:** if the new site does exactly one thing brilliantly, what is it?
   (e.g. book discovery calls, sell the course, make hiring easier)
3. **Success in 6 months:** what number or feeling tells the client it worked?
4. **Must-have pages/sections:** anything non-negotiable, incl. content that
   must carry over from the current site.
5. **Must-NOT:** anything the client refuses (styles, words, features, colors).
6. **Existing material:** current site URL, copy docs, photography — what
   exists and what's usable? Drop files in `pipeline/inputs/`.
7. **Integrations:** booking, email list, CRM, payments, anything the site
   must talk to.
8. **Domain & hosting status:** who owns it, where it lives, who has access.
9. **Deadline & drivers:** launch date and what's behind it (event, campaign,
   season)?
10. **Approval chain:** who reviews, who has final say, expected turnaround
    on feedback.

## C. Research seeds — [NEW — optional but valuable]
*Anything to point step 1 (Dig/Research) at:*

- Competitors the client admires or fears (beyond the brand-kit list)
- Search phrases customers actually say/type
- Questions customers ask again and again (gold for AEO)
- Reviews/testimonials worth mining for language
