# Studio Profile — fill this in ONCE

> **What this is:** the standing preferences of the studio — everything that is
> true for EVERY project. Every pipeline skill reads this file before working,
> so anything written here never has to be repeated in a questionnaire, a
> revision note, or a chat message again. Edit it only when the methodology
> evolves.
>
> **How to fill it in:** replace each ✏️ block with your own words. Short and
> honest beats long and aspirational — skills follow what's written here
> literally. Delete any section that doesn't apply.

## 1. Studio identity & voice
✏️ *How do you talk about your studio and your work? What tone do your
deliverables carry — warm, direct, playful, premium? Write 3–5 sentences the
way you'd say them, plus 2–3 phrases you'd NEVER use.*

## 2. Design taste & patterns
✏️ *What does a "Sarah site" look like? Preferred layout patterns (hero styles,
section rhythms, navigation), typography leanings, whitespace philosophy,
what you consider dated or off-brand. Name 2–3 live sites (yours or others')
that represent the taste, with one line on why each.*

## 3. Tech stack & build preferences
✏️ *What do you actually build with? (e.g. Webflow / Framer / Next.js /
WordPress — plus hosting, forms, analytics, CMS choices.) Build-spec documents
(step 5) will be written FOR this stack, in its vocabulary. Note anything you
refuse to build with.*

## 4. Writing & content rules
✏️ *Rules for page copy: reading level, sentence length habits, US/UK English,
how salesy is too salesy, how you handle CTAs, words to avoid. Content drafts
(step 3) apply these on top of each client's own brand voice.*

## 5. SEO / AEO standards
✏️ *Your non-negotiables: metadata conventions, heading discipline, schema
markup you always include, the AEO bar a page must clear before you'd ship it.
(The QA checklist in step 6 already covers the generic baseline — write only
what YOU add on top.)*

## 6. QA & launch bar
✏️ *What must be true before you call a site launch-ready? Device/browser
matrix you test, performance thresholds, accessibility bar, the small details
you always catch (favicons, 404 page, form success states…).*

## 7. Deliverable formats
✏️ *How you like documents structured: bullet-heavy vs prose, tables or not,
how long a Dig Brief should feel, whether specs include time estimates,
anything about how artifacts should be formatted for YOUR review speed.*

## 8. Business rules
✏️ *Anything commercial the pipeline should respect beyond the built-in
2-revisions-per-step rule: what counts as scope creep, what triggers a "this
is a change order" flag, standard exclusions.*
