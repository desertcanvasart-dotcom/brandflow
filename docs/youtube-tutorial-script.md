Agency Beats consolidates all of that into one intelligent platform. You manage campaigns, collaborate with clients, track performance, and leverage AI — without switching between apps.

After signup, you land on the Dashboard — the command center for your entire operation. Let's walk through what you see here.

## CHAPTER 2 — THE DASHBOARD (2:30–4:00)

**[SCREEN: /dashboard — pan across the full page]**

**NARRATOR:**
The dashboard gives you an at-a-glance view of everything happening across your agency.

At the top, you have four stat cards: total projects, total team members, total tasks, and overdue tasks — that last one lights up red when something needs attention.

**[SCREEN: Hover over the task breakdown section]**

**NARRATOR:**
Below that, a task breakdown shows how many tasks are completed, in progress, and overdue. Next to it, your six most recent tasks with their status and due dates — click any one to jump straight into it.

**[SCREEN: Scroll to meetings and activity feed]**

**NARRATOR:**
Further down, you see upcoming meetings and a real-time activity feed showing every action your team has taken — task updates, comments, content changes — all in chronological order.

Now let's set up a brand.

## CHAPTER 3 — BRAND MANAGEMENT (4:00–6:30)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to /brands → click "New Brand"]**

**NARRATOR:**
In Agency Beats, everything revolves around brands. Each client you manage gets their own brand profile with its own assets, guidelines, contacts, and content.

Let's create one. Give it a name, add a description, upload the logo, select which platforms this brand is active on — Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok, YouTube, whatever they use — and drop in their website URL.

**[SCREEN: Save → navigate to brand detail page]**

**NARRATOR:**
Once created, the brand detail page has five tabs.

**[SCREEN: Click through each tab]**

**NARRATOR:**
**Overview** — the high-level summary.
**Contacts** — your client-side points of contact. Add their name, email, phone.
**Guidelines** — brand tone of voice, color palettes, typography, dos and don'ts. This is what AI agents reference when generating content.
**Assets** — upload logos, images, templates, fonts — everything the team needs.
And **Client Access** — control which external users can see this brand through the client portal.

Now that we have a brand, let's create a project.

---

## CHAPTER 4 — PROJECT MANAGEMENT (6:30–9:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to /projects → click "New Project"]**

**NARRATOR:**
Projects come in three types. **Content Operations** for recurring social and blog campaigns. **Web Build** for website development. And **Full Service** for clients where you're doing both.

Select a type, link it to the brand we just created, give it a name, set a start and end date, and you're ready to go.

**[SCREEN: Navigate to project detail page — show the tab bar]**

**NARRATOR:**
Inside a project, the tabs adapt to the project type. Content projects get a Kanban board and content calendar. Web projects get a phase tracker and Gantt timeline. Full service projects get everything.

Let's look at the Kanban board — this is where the day-to-day work happens.

**[SCREEN: Click "Board" tab → show Kanban columns]**

**NARRATOR:**
The board uses a ten-stage pipeline. Tasks flow from Backlog through To Do, In Progress, In Review, Client Review, Approved, Scheduled, Published, and Done. There's also a Blocked column for anything that's stuck.

Each card shows the task title, who it's assigned to, the due date, priority level, and platform badge. Drag cards between columns to update status — it saves automatically.

**[SCREEN: Open a task card → show the detail panel]**

**NARRATOR:**
Click into any task and you get the full detail panel. Assign or reassign team members, set due dates, add comments with @mentions, attach files, and link deliverables. Everything your team needs is right here — no switching to Slack or email.

---

## CHAPTER 5 — CONTENT CALENDAR & GANTT (9:00–11:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to /calendar]**

**NARRATOR:**
The content calendar gives you a monthly view of everything that's scheduled. Color-coded dots show status at a glance. Filter by brand if you're managing multiple clients. Click any date to see what's planned or to add new content.

**[SCREEN: Navigate to /timeline]**

**NARRATOR:**
For a broader view, the Gantt timeline shows task durations as horizontal bars across a scrollable timeline. A blue marker shows today's date. Color coding matches task status — blue for in progress, green for done, red for blocked.

Switch between day, week, and month granularity using the view controls. And filter by project if you want to focus on a specific client.

---

## CHAPTER 6 — PUBLISHING QUEUE (11:00–12:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to /queue]**

**NARRATOR:**
Once content is approved, it moves to the publishing queue. This is a table view showing every piece of content that's ready to go — filtered by brand, platform, or status.

For each item, you can schedule a publish date and time, mark it as published when it goes live, or unschedule if plans change. If the content is already live, you can link directly to the published URL.

## CHAPTER 7 — MEETINGS & VIDEO CONFERENCING (12:00–15:00)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to /meetings]**

**NARRATOR:**
Agency Beats has built-in HD video conferencing — no Zoom or Google Meet needed. It's powered by LiveKit with AI transcription from Deepgram.

**[SCREEN: Create a new meeting]**

**NARRATOR:**
Create a meeting with a title, type, date, time, and duration. Link it to a project and brand for context.

**[SCREEN: Enter a meeting room → show video interface]**

**NARRATOR:**
In the meeting room, you get real-time HD video and audio, screen sharing, and a participant grid. On the right side, a live transcription sidebar captures everything being said — powered by Deepgram AI.

**[SCREEN: Show meeting detail tabs — Transcript, AI]**

**NARRATOR:**
After the meeting, the transcript tab has the full text. But here's where it gets powerful. The AI tab gives you two tools: a **Meeting Summarizer** that auto-generates a structured summary, and a **Brief Generator** that extracts action items and service briefs from the conversation.

**[SCREEN: Show Transcript Chat]**

**NARRATOR:**
There's also Transcript Chat — a RAG-powered search across your meeting history. Ask it "What did the client say about the homepage design?" and it searches every transcript to find the answer. It can even create tasks or add items to briefs directly from the conversation.

**[SCREEN: Show guest meeting link]**

**NARRATOR:**
Need a client to join? Share a guest link. They enter their name and email on a pre-join screen and participate in the call without needing an Agency Beats account. Perfect for client kickoffs and reviews.

## CHAPTER 8 — INTAKE & SERVICE BRIEFS (15:00–17:30)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to project → Intake tab]**

**NARRATOR:**
This is one of the most powerful features. After a client meeting, AI extracts a structured intake record from the transcript — client name, project scope, services discussed, budget, timeline, and requirements.

**[SCREEN: Show extracted intake → service briefs]**

**NARRATOR:**
From that intake, the system generates individual service briefs — one for each service discussed. There are ten brief types: Website, SEO, Content, Social Media, Paid Ads, Email Marketing, Branding, CRO, Analytics, and Strategy. Each brief has fields specific to that service.

**[SCREEN: Show AI task generation from a completed brief]**

**NARRATOR:**
When a brief is approved and marked complete, you can generate tasks from it. AI compares the brief requirements against over 120 task templates in the Task Library. It includes or excludes each template with a clear reason — so you understand exactly why each task was suggested.

You review the list, add any excluded tasks you want, and import them into your project. In about thirty seconds, you go from a client conversation to a fully scoped task board.

## CHAPTER 9 — AI AGENTS HUB (17:30–19:30)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to /ai]**

**NARRATOR:**
The AI Agents hub is your marketing intelligence center. Six specialized tools, all powered by Anthropic's Claude API.

**[SCREEN: Click through each agent]**

**NARRATOR:**
**Ad Copy Generator** — select a platform like Meta or LinkedIn, describe what you're promoting, and get platform-optimized ad variations in seconds.

**CTA Suggestions** — paste your landing page context and get high-converting call-to-action variations with A/B testing recommendations.

**SEO Research** — enter a topic or keyword and get opportunity analysis, content gaps, and a strategy outline.

**Competitor Analysis** — describe a competitor and get a positioning comparison with content strategy insights.

**Performance Report** — feed in your analytics data and get an AI-written report with insights and recommendations. No more manual spreadsheet reports.

**Brand Strategy** — define brand pillars, personas, tone, and objectives with AI guidance.

**[SCREEN: Brief pause on the AI response panel]**

**NARRATOR:**
Every agent in the hub draws from your Knowledge Base. That means the more brand documents, past campaigns, and reference materials you upload, the smarter and more relevant the AI outputs become.

Which brings us to the Knowledge Base.

## CHAPTER 10 — KNOWLEDGE BASE (19:30–22:30)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to /knowledge-base]**

**NARRATOR:**
The Knowledge Base is the brain behind every AI agent. This is where you store everything your AI needs to generate content that actually sounds like your brand.

**[SCREEN: Show the upload dialog with different options]**

**NARRATOR:**
You can add content four ways. **Upload a file** — PDF, text, markdown, or CSV up to 50 megabytes. **Paste text** directly — meeting notes, research, anything. **Import from a URL** — paste a webpage link and the content is automatically fetched and indexed. Or use **Structured Forms**.

**[SCREEN: Open a structured form — show Persona type]**

**NARRATOR:**
Structured forms are purpose-built templates for the types of documents agencies create most often. There are five types: Persona, Brand Strategy, Competitor, Campaign, and SOP. Each has specific fields — like goals, pain points, and preferred channels for a persona.

What makes this special is that each field is individually embedded. So when an AI agent needs to know a persona's pain points specifically, it pulls just that field — not the entire document. Fine-grained context means more relevant outputs.

**[SCREEN: Click the Auto-Fill sparkles button → show the popover]**

**NARRATOR:**
Now here's the time saver. Click "Auto-fill from document" on any structured form. Upload a PDF, paste a URL, or type raw text — and AI extracts the relevant fields automatically. Fields filled by AI show an amber highlight so you know to review them. Edit any field and the highlight clears.

**[SCREEN: Show CSV import flow]**

**NARRATOR:**
For bulk data, there's CSV import. Upload a spreadsheet and the system auto-maps your columns to form fields. Adjust any mismatches, preview the first five rows, and confirm. Records are created in bulk with a progress bar.

**[SCREEN: Show HubSpot import option]**

**NARRATOR:**
If you're using HubSpot, connect via OAuth and import contacts directly as persona documents. The system pulls contacts with job titles and company information — ready to use as structured personas.

**[SCREEN: Show version history panel (Clock icon)]**

**NARRATOR:**
Every time you save a document, a version snapshot is created automatically. Open the version history panel to see a timeline of all changes — with auto-generated summaries like "Updated: Goals, Pain Points." Preview any version with a side-by-side diff, and restore a previous version with one click. Restoring is non-destructive — it creates a new version, so nothing is ever lost.

**[SCREEN: Show presence avatars and field locks]**

**NARRATOR:**
Multiple team members can edit the same document at the same time. You'll see presence avatars showing who's viewing, and field-level indicators showing who's actively editing each field. If two people edit the same field, a conflict toast lets you choose which version to keep.

**[SCREEN: Show share dialog]**

**NARRATOR:**
Finally, any knowledge document can be shared externally via a public link. Optionally add a password and set an expiry date. View count tracking shows you how many times it's been accessed. And if you need to revoke access, one click disables the link.

## CHAPTER 11 — TEAM, NOTIFICATIONS & CLIENT PORTAL (22:30–24:30)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to /team]**

**NARRATOR:**
Team management uses a six-role hierarchy. Admin, Manager, Creator, Developer, Viewer, and Client. Each role has specific permissions — Admins can manage settings and billing, Creators can produce content, Clients only see the portal.

Invite new members by email, assign their role, and optionally set a department.

**[SCREEN: Navigate to /settings → show notification preferences grid]**

**NARRATOR:**
Notifications are fully customizable. Agency Beats delivers across five channels — in-app, email, push notifications, Slack, and webhooks. You choose which events trigger notifications on which channels. There's a grid interface — seven event types across five channels, each with its own toggle.

Set quiet hours so your team isn't disturbed outside work hours. And choose between individual emails or daily or weekly digests.

**[SCREEN: Brief view of client portal]**

**NARRATOR:**
For your clients, there's a branded portal where they can review content, leave feedback with inline comments, track project status in real time, join meetings, and access shared knowledge documents. All without needing a full Agency Beats account.

## CHAPTER 12 — SETTINGS & INTEGRATIONS (24:30–25:30)

**[SCREEN: Navigate to /settings → scroll through integrations]**

In Settings, you'll find integrations for Figma — sync design files and add visual annotations. Slack — send notifications to your workspace channels. Webhooks — push events to any custom endpoint with signed payloads. HubSpot — connect your CRM for persona imports. Gmail and Outlook for email sync. And Google Calendar for event visibility.

There's also an API key section for custom integrations, auto-assignment rules for automatic task routing, and a full audit log of every action taken in your organization.
